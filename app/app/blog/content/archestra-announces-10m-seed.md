## What changed in ten months

When we wrote the [pre-seed post](/blog/archestra-unveils-open-source-solution-announces-vc-backing), we said enterprises would push hard on AI agents and immediately run into security and compliance walls. That's exactly what happened — just faster and more painfully than I expected.

The first wave of AI pilots has already wrapped up across the Fortune 500, and a lot of teams are arriving at the same uncomfortable conclusion: **scaling agents isn't a model problem. It's an infrastructure problem.** You can have the best model in the world, but if you can't connect it to your data without risking exfiltration, you've got an expensive demo, not a deployment.

That's the gap we've been filling. Archestra is a horizontal platform that lets in-house AI teams open up data access to business users without the usual nightmare scenarios. It plugs into the AI tools you already use, deploys in days, and gives you deterministic guardrails, governance, and observability out of the box.

Until now, teams have had to choose between locking agents down so hard they're useless, or letting them loose and praying. We don't think that's a real choice. **Our platform guarantees 100% protection against data leaks while actually accelerating agent development.** Both things, at once. That's the whole bet.

The other thing I underestimated was how fast the open-source community would show up:
